# The One Minute Manager

**Authors:** Ken Blanchard & Spencer Johnson  
**First published:** 1982  
**Era:** Foundational — the management classic that crosses into sales leadership

---

## Why It Matters

A 100-page parable that has shaped how millions of managers run teams. For sales **managers**, dealership owners, agency leaders, and team builders in PR — this is the operating system.

The book's lessons matter even for the individual contributor, because **managing yourself** is the precondition for managing anyone else.

---

## Core Thesis

Great managers do three things, consistently, in under a minute each. Anything more elaborate is usually busywork.

---

## The Three Secrets

### 1. One Minute Goals

- Agree on goals together.
- Write each goal on a single page, in 250 words or less.
- Each goal can be read in under a minute.
- Re-read goals daily to stay aligned with what matters.
- *"Take a minute, look at your goals, look at your performance, see if your behavior matches your goals."*

### 2. One Minute Praisings

Catch people doing something *right* — not wrong.

- Praise immediately.
- Be specific about what they did.
- Share how it made you feel.
- Pause to let the feeling land.
- Encourage them to keep doing it.
- Make clear you support and trust them.

### 3. One Minute Re-Directs (originally "Reprimands")

When someone falls short:

- Address it immediately. No grudges, no banked complaints.
- Be specific about what went wrong.
- Share how it made you feel.
- Pause.
- Remind them they are better than this, that you value them, and that the *behavior* is the problem, not the person.
- End with reaffirmation.

*(In later editions, Blanchard renamed this the "Re-Direct" to emphasize forward motion over punishment.)*

---

## The Manager's Mantra

> **People who feel good about themselves produce good results.**

Every technique in the book serves this single thesis.

---

## PR Application Notes

- **Goals on one page.** In a dealership or call center: every rep should have a single-page weekly goal card. Calls, appointments, closes, average ticket. Visible. Updated daily.
- **Immediate praising.** Boricua culture is warm and verbal — immediate, specific recognition lands powerfully. *"Eso que hiciste con la señora Rivera, cómo manejaste su preocupación con el seguro, eso fue de campeón."*
- **Re-directs in private, never in public.** Save face is a non-negotiable principle in PR workplaces. The Blanchard model already separates *behavior* from *person* — culturally essential.
- **The 1-minute rhythm fits the floor.** Managers who try to schedule weekly 1-hour 1:1s often fail because the day is too dynamic. 60-second praisings and re-directs *as they happen* keep the team calibrated.

---

## Companion Concept: Situational Leadership

Blanchard later expanded the 1MM framework into *Situational Leadership II*, which says managers must flex their style based on the **development level** of the employee on a *specific task*:

| Development Level | Manager Style |
|---|---|
| D1 – Enthusiastic Beginner (low competence, high commitment) | **S1 Directing** — tell them exactly what to do |
| D2 – Disillusioned Learner (low/some competence, low commitment) | **S2 Coaching** — explain *why*, support emotionally |
| D3 – Capable but Cautious (moderate/high competence, variable commitment) | **S3 Supporting** — facilitate decisions, encourage |
| D4 – Self-Reliant Achiever (high competence, high commitment) | **S4 Delegating** — hand it off, get out of the way |

This matters because the biggest mistake new sales managers make is **applying S4 (delegating) to a D1 (new rep)**, then blaming the rep when results fail.

## Where The 1MM Shows Up Elsewhere

- **Hormozi** repeatedly emphasizes single-page metrics dashboards — pure Blanchard.
- **Holmes's** *Ultimate Sales Machine* applies one-page goals to organizational pig-headed discipline.
- **Ross's** *Predictable Revenue* role specialization assumes a manager who can coach against task-specific competence — Situational Leadership in disguise.

## Action Items

- [ ] Write your top 3 weekly goals on a single index card. Keep it on your desk.
- [ ] Catch one team member doing one thing right today. Praise it in under a minute, specifically.
- [ ] When the next issue comes up, run the re-direct in three steps: behavior → feeling → reaffirmation. Total time: under 60 seconds.
