# Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion

**Author:** Robert B. Cialdini, PhD  
**First published:** 1984 (expanded edition 2021 adds the 7th principle)  
**Era:** Foundational — the academic spine of modern sales psychology

---

## Why It Matters

The most cited book on influence ever written. Cialdini spent three years undercover in compliance professions — car lots, fundraising boiler rooms, sales schools — cataloging what *actually* works. He then validated each principle with academic experiments.

Every modern sales psychologist — Voss, Hormozi, Robbins — quotes Cialdini. **You cannot ethically sell at a high level without knowing these principles, and you cannot defend yourself against unethical operators without recognizing them.**

---

## Core Thesis

Humans make most decisions on **autopilot** — click, whirr — using mental shortcuts called *fixed-action patterns*. Skilled influencers (and manipulators) trigger these patterns deliberately. The book identifies seven primary triggers.

---

## The 7 Principles (originally 6, with Unity added in 2021)

### 1. Reciprocity
We feel obligated to repay favors, gifts, and concessions. Give first — a sample, a free consultation, useful information, a small concession — and the prospect feels pulled to give back.

- **Door-in-the-face**: Make a large request, then retreat to your real (smaller) ask. The retreat is read as a concession, triggering reciprocal concession.
- **PR application**: A free vehicle inspection before the test drive. A free policy review before the insurance pitch. *Café y agua* at the dealership — small but real.

### 2. Commitment & Consistency
Once we commit — especially in writing, publicly, with effort — we feel internal pressure to behave consistently with that commitment.

- **Foot-in-the-door**: Tiny initial "yes" predicts large later "yes."
- **Carnegie's tie-down chain** runs on this exact principle.
- **PR application**: Get them to articulate, out loud, what matters to them. *"Así que lo más importante es la seguridad de los nenes, ¿verdad?"* Their own words become the script that closes them.

### 3. Social Proof
We look to what others are doing, especially others *like us*, to decide what's correct.

- The more uncertain the situation, the heavier the influence of social proof.
- **Similar others** beat dissimilar others. A Bayamón family is moved by other Bayamón families more than by Miami testimonials.
- **PR application**: Reviews, photos, and video testimonials — *en español, con acento boricua* — outperform polished gringo testimonials 10:1 in PR markets.

### 4. Liking
We say yes to people we like. Likeability comes from:
- **Physical attractiveness** (halo effect)
- **Similarity** (background, dress, hobbies, accent)
- **Compliments**
- **Familiarity / contact**
- **Cooperation toward shared goals**
- **Association** with positive things (the meal at a meeting, the music in the showroom)

This principle is **Carnegie distilled into a single word.**

### 5. Authority
We defer to credible experts. Titles, uniforms, trappings of status — even when irrelevant — increase compliance.

- **Expertness + trustworthiness** is the dual signal. Cialdini's research shows that *briefly admitting a small downside* of your product before describing its benefits **dramatically increases trustworthiness** — and the strength of the close.
- **PR application**: Display certifications, awards, years in business, partnerships. Then — admit the one small thing your product *isn't* perfect at, before pitching what it *is* perfect at.

### 6. Scarcity
We want what is rare, disappearing, or restricted.

- **Deadline + limited supply** is the most reliable scarcity combo.
- **Loss framing** beats gain framing: *"You'll miss out on $400 if we don't lock it today"* > *"You'll save $400 if we lock it today."*
- **PR application**: Real deadlines (end-of-month manufacturer programs, year-end tax windows) work. Fake deadlines work *once* and burn the relationship for life.

### 7. Unity (added 2021)
Shared *identity* — family, tribe, region, religion, profession — outweighs ordinary similarity. "We" beats "like me."

- **PR application**: This principle is *enormous* in PR. "Familia Boricua" framing, supporting the local economy, hometown loyalty — these are not slogans, they are the strongest persuasion lever available in the market. Use them honestly or not at all.

---

## How to Use This Ethically

Cialdini draws a sharp line: 
- **Detectives** use these tools to surface true needs and present honest value with appropriate emphasis. 
- **Smugglers** use them to push junk past defenses. 

Smugglers win short. Detectives win careers. PR is a small island — reputation is everything. Be a detective.

---

## Where Cialdini Shows Up Elsewhere

- **Hormozi's** Grand Slam Offer leans on Scarcity + Urgency + Bonuses (reciprocity).
- **Voss** uses Liking via tactical empathy and Authority via the "late-night FM DJ voice."
- **Hopkins's** tie-downs are pure Commitment & Consistency.
- **Ziglar** taught Reciprocity decades before Cialdini named it.
