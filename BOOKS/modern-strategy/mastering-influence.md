# Mastering Influence

**Author / Creator:** Tony Robbins  
**Format:** Audio program (Robbins Research International)  
**First released:** 1990s; multiple updated editions  
**Era:** Modern strategy — the psychology-of-persuasion bridge between Cialdini and modern closing

---

## Why It Matters

*Mastering Influence* is the most widely circulated sales-psychology audio program of the past three decades. Robbins took the academic findings of Cialdini, the field techniques of Ziglar/Hopkins, and the **state-management** discipline of his NLP roots and welded them into a 10-day operator's curriculum.

It is the single most-cited audio program among modern high-performing sales operators because it teaches *the state you must be in to apply every other technique.*

---

## Core Thesis

Influence is **state plus skill.** Skill without state — a trained closer in a tired, distracted, low-conviction state — fails. State without skill — a passionate believer who can't run discovery — also fails. The pros run *both,* on demand, every interaction.

The shortcut: master your own physiology and focus first; the techniques then work because they're delivered through a peak-state operator.

## Key Concepts

### 1. State Management
Your state in the next 60 seconds is engineered, not stumbled into. Three levers:

- **Physiology** — posture, breathing, movement, facial expression. Move differently, feel differently.
- **Focus** — what you direct attention at. "Where focus goes, energy flows."
- **Language** — the words you say (internally and aloud) shape state in real time.

A Robbins pre-call ritual is typically 30–60 seconds of physical movement, breathing, and a stated outcome — conducted *before* every important interaction.

### 2. The Six Human Needs
Robbins's psychological model. Every buyer (and every seller) is driven by some mix of:

1. **Certainty** — the need to avoid pain, feel safe, know what's coming.
2. **Variety / Uncertainty** — the need for novelty, stimulation, surprise.
3. **Significance** — the need to feel important, unique, valuable.
4. **Love / Connection** — the need to feel close, accepted, part of something.
5. **Growth** — the need to develop, expand, become more.
6. **Contribution** — the need to give beyond oneself.

The first four are *personality* needs (everyone has all four, in different orders). The last two are *spiritual* needs (developed over time). 

**Sales application:** discover which 2 needs dominate your prospect, and frame the offer to satisfy *both*. A buyer high on Certainty + Significance buys differently from one high on Variety + Connection.

### 3. Pattern Interrupt
When a prospect goes into objection mode, they're running a familiar mental script. Interrupt the pattern — a question, a non-sequitur, a posture change, a laugh — and the script can't complete. Then re-engage in a different frame.

Voss's tactical empathy uses a calmer version of the same principle.

### 4. Anchoring
A specific physical/sensory cue (a touch, a phrase, a posture) becomes paired with a desired state. Sellers anchor their own peak state. Trainers anchor confidence in their reps. Used ethically, anchors are state shortcuts.

### 5. Future Pacing
Guide the prospect to *mentally inhabit* their post-purchase reality. "Imagine it's six months from now, you've had the system installed, and the team is reporting on time, every Monday morning. What's the first thing that changes for you?" 

The brain processes vividly imagined futures with the same machinery as remembered pasts. By the end of a well-run future pace, the prospect *feels* like the decision is already made.

### 6. Reframing
The meaning of any event is a frame, and frames can be changed.

- **Content reframe:** same event, different meaning. ("It's not expensive, it's an investment in safety for the family.")
- **Context reframe:** same event, different setting. ("In a market that's rising, paying a premium today is the cheapest entry point you'll see this year.")

Reframing is the single most powerful objection-handling tool in the Robbins arsenal.

### 7. The Power of Conviction
Robbins is relentless on this: *the seller's belief in the product, in the company, and in themselves is the single largest determinant of close rate.* No script, no technique substitutes. If you cannot deliver the offer in a state of complete conviction, **work on the conviction first.**

## The Robbins Sale Sequence (synthesized)

1. **State first.** Pre-call ritual. Peak physiology, focused attention, set outcome.
2. **Rapport.** Pace and lead — match their tempo, energy, language; then guide the rhythm.
3. **Discovery via the Six Needs.** What do they really value, and what do they fear?
4. **Future pace the dream outcome.** Vivid, sensory, present-tense.
5. **Present** the solution as the bridge between the current pain and the future pace.
6. **Reframe** any objection before answering it.
7. **Close** with conviction, no hesitation, no apology.
8. **Anchor** the positive decision — a phrase, a celebration, a handshake — so the post-purchase remorse window stays closed.

## PR Application Notes

- **State management is the single biggest leverage point** for PR sellers operating in high-rejection environments (auto, insurance, telecom retail). The seller who runs 8 hours at peak state outperforms the seller running 4 hours at peak + 4 hours at half-mast by a factor most managers underestimate.
- **The Six Needs map cleanly to PR cultural archetypes.** Many traditional Boricua buyers index high on *Certainty + Connection* (family-centered, risk-averse). Younger urban buyers often index higher on *Variety + Significance.* Adjust the pitch's emotional center accordingly.
- **Future pacing works exceptionally well in Spanish.** *"Imagínate, en seis meses, los nenes en su nueva escuela, el carro en el driveway, los sábados libres porque ya no tienes que estar pendiente del taller…"* Try this once. You'll see why Robbins built a billion-dollar business on this technique.
- **Reframing** is a culturally familiar form of conversation in PR — *"Piénselo así…"* is a near-direct linguistic equivalent of "let me reframe."
