## Why It Matters

*Offers* solved the conversion problem. *Leads* solves the **traffic** problem. Together they form the most complete two-book sales-and-marketing framework of the modern era.

Hormozi's premise: an A+ offer with no leads is a private joke. An A+ leads engine with a B+ offer prints money. Both are required, in that order — you build the offer first, then you build the engine that feeds it.

---

## Core Thesis

There are exactly **four core ways** to get leads, and **four amplifiers** that turn those four cores into a leads machine. Every business needs at least three of the four core methods running simultaneously to be resilient.

## The Four Core Lead Sources

```
                Warm                     Cold
          ╔════════════════════════╦════════════════════════╗
   Free   ║  1. Warm Outreach     ║  2. Post Free Content ║
          ╠════════════════════════╬════════════════════════╣
   Paid   ║  3. Run Paid Ads      ║  4. Cold Outreach     ║
          ╚════════════════════════╩════════════════════════╝
```

### 1. Warm Outreach — People You Know
Text, call, message your existing network. Includes referrals, reactivations, and your phone's contact list. The cheapest, fastest lead source for any seller who hasn't worked it. Most underused.

### 2. Post Free Content — People Who Watch You
Social posts, video, podcasts, written content. Free to make, free to consume. The *long-game* lead source — slow to start, exponential at scale. Builds the *audience* that compounds for years.

### 3. Run Paid Ads — Strangers You Reach via Money
Meta, Google, YouTube, TikTok, programmatic. Predictable, scalable, instantly turn-on-able. The fastest path from zero to leads, *if* the offer and creative are strong.

### 4. Cold Outreach — Strangers You Reach Directly
Cold email, cold call, cold DM, door-knocking, walk-ins. Requires volume and discipline. **Underrated** in 2026 precisely because most operators won't do it.

## The Four Lead Amplifiers

Take the four cores and multiply each with one of these:

### A. Customer Referrals
Your existing customers → their network. The cheapest, highest-converting traffic that exists. Hormozi: most businesses leave 50%+ of their referral potential on the table by simply *never asking.*

### B. Employees
Every employee you hire — if structured right — multiplies any of the 4 cores. SDRs amplify cold outreach. Content team amplifies posting. Media buyers amplify ads.

### C. Agencies
A hired agency is a leased employee team. Lower commitment, higher cost per output, faster to start.

### D. Affiliates / Partners
Other people's audiences. Pay only on result. The single most asymmetric leverage available to a smaller operator.

## The Hormozi Lead Magnet Doctrine

A **lead magnet** is a free thing that solves a specific problem and earns contact info. To be effective it must:

1. Solve a *narrow, urgent, real* problem.
2. Be *instantly consumable* (under 10 minutes ideally).
3. Be *valuable enough you could charge for it* (and possibly do, briefly, before making it free).
4. *Create a natural sequel* — the next thing they need is your paid offer.

The lead magnet that gets traction shares a property with the Grand Slam Offer: **so good they feel stupid not opting in.**

## The Core Activity Math

Hormozi's rule of thumb for early-stage operators:

- **100 outreach actions per day** (warm + cold combined), every day.
- **3+ pieces of free content per week** at minimum.
- **Paid ads** turned on as soon as offer is validated.
- **Referrals** asked for at every successful interaction.

This is non-negotiable activity. The strategy is irrelevant if the activity isn't there.

## PR Application Notes

- **Warm outreach is enormous in PR.** Networks are dense; *confianza* travels through referral lines. A PR seller who systematically works *quinceañeras, bodas, bautizos,* church groups, sports clubs, and alumni associations is sitting on a goldmine of warm outreach.
- **Free content in Spanish is wide open.** Many PR niches have no dominant local creator. A consistent, useful Spanish-language content effort earns disproportionate trust.
- **Paid ads in PR are cheaper than the mainland US.** CPMs and CPAs run lower; well-targeted Meta and Google campaigns are accessible to smaller operators.
- **Cold calling still works in PR** — partly because so few sellers do it. The market hasn't been over-saturated the way US Tier 1 cities have.
- **Referral systems** must be culturally framed. *"Si conoces a alguien a quien podamos ayudar igual…"* lands better than transactional language about referral bonuses, although a real reward (gift card, free service, cash) still matters.
